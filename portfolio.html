The Problem

Why founders keep getting burned
by overseas manufacturing

90%

Production Has Gone Global

90% of North American manufacturers moved production internationally in the past five years — yet the trust infrastructure to support this shift is still broker-driven and opaque.

~55%

Most Fail Their Goals

Only ~55% of those companies hit their cost and speed objectives. For early-stage hardware founders with no institutional contacts, the odds are far worse.

The Solution

7 Degrees — trust built in

7 Degrees connects hardware companies with a network of verified global manufacturers, making overseas production trusted and accessible. From prototype to product.

01 —
Trust-Based Network

Every manufacturer is rigorously onboarded, continuously monitored, and strictly enforced. Violations get removed — no exceptions.

02 —
Reputation Profiles

Ratings, reviews, mutual connections, and verified certifications so founders know exactly who they're partnering with before committing.

03 —
Full Workflow

Discovery, matching, messaging, calls, IP-protected contracts, and ongoing quality monitoring — the entire partnership in one place.

Why Now

Three tailwinds converging

i.

Rise of Early-Stage Hardware

More founders are building consumer hardware and IoT products as the SaaS-pocalypse drives talent back to physical products.

Supply Chains Are Shifting

Companies are moving production from China to India and Vietnam — creating massive new sourcing demand without adequate trust infrastructure.

Trust Infrastructure Is Archaic

Sourcing is still driven by brokers and word of mouth. No modern, scalable, trust-first platform exists for early-stage hardware founders.
